The Herndon Home, GA

The Herndon Home is a historic house museum in Atlanta, GA. It was the home of Alonzo Franklin Herndon, a former slave who became Atlanta's first Black millionaire. The exquisite Beaux-Arts mansion was completed in 1910.

Located on Auburn Avenue in Atlanta, this Queen Anne style house was the birthplace and childhood home of civil rights leader Martin Luther King Jr. It stands as a powerful symbol of his origins and the community that shaped him.

About the Artist

Drawing from his professional life as an architect, Andrew Knight brings a unique structural understanding to his pen and ink illustrations. This deep appreciation for form, light, and precision informs his work, which focuses on architectural landmarks and moments of quiet contemplation.

Each piece is a testament to this dual passion, a labor of love composed of thousands of individual dots that build a rich tapestry of shadow and line. Welcome to his studio.
